23 why ethics

Why Ethics Matters in STS

Technology is never just technical. Every tech has social consequences. Ethics asks: Who benefits? Who's harmed? Who decides? Who's accountable?

Without ethics, tech serves power. With ethics, tech can serve justice.

23 definition

What is Ethics?

⚖️ Ethics IS

Philosophy of right/wrong. Reasoned frameworks for moral decisions.

🚫 Ethics is NOT

Not just law (legal ≠ ethical). Not religion. Not personal opinion.

💡 Ethics DOES

Helps decide when values conflict. Guides principled action.

23 frameworks

Ethical Frameworks

📊 Utilitarianism

Greatest good for the greatest number. Consequences matter most.

📜 Deontology

Duty-based. Some acts are inherently right or wrong regardless of outcome.

🏛️ Virtue Ethics

Character-based. What would a good, wise person do?

✊ Rights-Based

Every person has inherent rights that must not be violated.

❤️ Care Ethics

Relationships, responsibility, and empathy guide decisions.

🤔 Each gives

Different answers to the same dilemma.

23 applied example

Self-Driving Car Dilemma

The scenario

Car must choose: hit 1 pedestrian or hit 5. No safe option. Who does it save?

Utilitarian

Hit 1, save 5. Greatest good.

Deontological

Can't actively choose to kill. Duty not to murder.

Rights-based

The 1 person's right to life can't be overridden by math.

No easy answer. That's the point. Ethics gives you tools, not shortcuts.

23 consent

Is Digital Consent Real?

In theory

Users agree to terms. Companies disclose data use. Choice exists.

In reality

Nobody reads 50-page ToS. "Agree" is the only option. Kids and vulnerable users can't meaningfully consent.

Systemic ethical failure, not just individual carelessness.

📜✅🙈

Terms & Conditions: *47 pages*

User: "I have read and agree" (read nothing)

The entire digital economy runs on a legal fiction of consent.

23 responsibility gap

When AI Causes Harm, Who's Responsible?

Candidates

The programmer? The company? The user? The data provider? The algorithm itself?

The problem

Current law wasn't built for distributed algorithmic decisions. Accountability is diffused.

We need new accountability frameworks for the AI age.

24 the paradox

Technology: Cause AND Cure

🏭 Tech as CAUSE

Industrial pollution, fossil fuels, plastic, deforestation, e-waste, carbon emissions.

🌱 Tech as CURE

Renewables, carbon capture, precision agriculture, recycling, monitoring, EVs.

Not tech vs nature. It's WHICH tech, HOW deployed, governed by WHOM.

24 climate change

Climate Change & Technology

🌡️ The problem

Rising temps, sea level rise, extreme weather, food insecurity. PH is highly vulnerable.

💡 Tech solutions

Solar, wind, EVs, carbon capture, smart grids, precision agriculture, monitoring.

PH contributes little to emissions but suffers the most. Climate justice is an STS issue.

24 e-waste

E-Waste: Hidden Cost of Tech

📊 The numbers

50M+ tons globally per year. PH: 380,000+ tons. Toxic: lead, mercury, cadmium.

🔄 The system

Planned obsolescence: products designed to break. Informal recycling harms workers.

24 biodiversity

Biodiversity & Tech Solutions

🌿 PH is megadiverse

One of 18 most biodiverse countries. But species disappearing from habitat loss.

🛰️ Tech solutions

Satellite monitoring, DNA barcoding, drone surveys, AI wildlife tracking. Combined with indigenous knowledge.

🥤🌊😭

Humans 1907: "Plastic! So convenient!"

Humans 2024: "It's in our blood now. Help."

Innovation without foresight = crisis. STS = think long-term BEFORE adoption.

24 future generations

Do We Owe Future Generations?

Future generations will inherit our environmental decisions. They can't vote or negotiate. But they'll live with consequences of OUR tech choices. Sustainability is an ethical obligation, not just a preference.
