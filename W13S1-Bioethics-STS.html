03 Framework

4 Principles of Bioethics

1. Autonomy

Respecting individual choice and self-determination

2. Beneficence

Acting for the good of the patient/subject

3. Non-maleficence

"First, do no harm" — avoiding damage

4. Justice

Fair distribution of benefits and burdens

Frankenfood or Future of Farming?

05 Definition

What Exactly Are GMOs?

Organisms whose genetic material has been artificially altered using genetic engineering techniques.

❌ NOT this:

Traditional breeding / selection

✅ THIS:

Direct DNA manipulation in a lab

Famous GMO Examples

🍅

Flavr Savr Tomato

05 The Case FOR

GMO Pros ✅

  • Increases food production to feed growing population (10B by 2050)
  • Reduces need for chemical pesticides and herbicides
  • Can be biofortified with vitamins (Golden Rice = Vitamin A)
  • Drought/flood resistant crops for climate change adaptation
  • Over 3,000+ studies confirm safety for human consumption
05 The Case AGAINST

GMO Cons ❌

  • Long-term effects on health still debated by some
  • Corporate control of food supply (Monsanto/Bayer patents)
  • Environmental risks: cross-pollination, superweeds
  • Farmers become dependent on seed companies (no replanting)
  • Cultural & religious objections to "playing God"

06 Types

3 Types of Cloning

🧪

Gene Cloning

Copying DNA segments to produce proteins

🐑

Reproductive Cloning

Creating a whole identical organism

❤️‍🩹

Therapeutic Cloning

Creating stem cells for treatment

06 Case Study

Dolly the Sheep (1996)

🐑

Born: July 5, 1996 (Scotland)

Method: Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer

Died: Age 6 (premature aging)

Legacy: Proved mammal cloning possible

Key Takeaway

277 attempts → 1 success. Dolly developed arthritis and lung disease prematurely. Cloning works, but at what cost?

06 The Big Question

Should We Clone Humans?

Arguments FOR

• Infertility solution

• Organ farming (therapeutic)

• Preserve endangered species

• Scientific progress

Arguments AGAINST

• Violates human dignity

• Identity/psychological issues

• No informed consent possible

• High failure/deformity rate

07 Definition

What Are Designer Babies?

Children whose genetic characteristics have been artificially selected or altered before birth.

METHOD 1: PGD

Select embryos with desired traits

METHOD 2: Gene Editing

Directly modify embryo DNA with CRISPR

07 The Spectrum

Where Do We Draw the Line?

ACCEPTED

GRAY AREA

CONTROVERSIAL

Select sex, prevent disabilities

Choose eye color, height, IQ

07 Case Study

The CRISPR Baby Scandal (2018)

Who: He Jiankui, Chinese biophysicist

What: CRISPR on human embryos (twins Lulu & Nana)

Goal: HIV resistance (CCR5 gene deletion)

Result: Global condemnation, 3-year prison

⚠️

FIRST GENE-EDITED
BABIES BORN

07 Arguments For

Designer Baby Pros ✅

  • Eliminate hereditary diseases (cancer, Huntington's, CF)
  • Reduce suffering for future generations
  • Personalized medicine from birth
  • Parents' right to give children the best start
  • Natural extension of prenatal care and IVF
07 Arguments Against

Designer Baby Cons ❌

  • Creates genetic class divide (rich vs. poor DNA)
  • Loss of human genetic diversity
  • Child cannot consent to modifications
  • Off-target CRISPR effects may appear years later
  • Slippery slope to eugenics

09 Summary

  • Bioethics = biology meets moral philosophy
