02Definition

What is the Digital Divide?

The gap between those who have access to modern ICT and those who don't — including differences in skills and outcomes.

Not just "Do you have WiFi?" — it's deeper than that.

02Connection to STS

Why It's an STS Issue

Technology that should equalize actually widens inequality.

• Education moved online → those without internet fall behind

• Jobs require digital skills → no access = no opportunity

• Government services go digital → unconnected citizens excluded

02Framework

3 Levels of Digital Divide

📡

Level 1: ACCESS

Do you have internet and devices?

🧠

Level 2: USAGE

Do you have skills to use it effectively?

📈

Level 3: OUTCOMES

Does it actually improve your life?

04The Gap 🇵🇭

PH Digital Divide: Urban vs Rural

🏙️ Metro Manila / Urban

• 90%+ internet access

• Fiber speeds: 100+ Mbps

• Multiple ISP options

🏔️ Rural / Provincial

• Under 50% in BARMM, Caraga

• Mobile only: 3-5 Mbps on good days

• Often 1 telco (no competition)

Same country. Completely different digital realities.

04COVID Impact

The Pandemic Exposed It All

COVID forced everything online overnight:

📚 Schools → Online classes (no internet = no education)

💼 Work → WFH (no connectivity = no income)

🏥 Health → Telemedicine (no access = no healthcare)

🏛️ Government → Online services (no device = excluded)

04Student Reality

Student Struggles During Online Learning

🏔️ Students climbed mountains just to get signal

📱 3 siblings shared 1 phone for their classes

💰 Used PHP 10 data promos for 3-hour lectures

📄 Teachers delivered printed modules door-to-door

📊 Result: 3.2 million students dropped out

04Speed

PH Internet Speed Ranking

Behind: 🇻🇳 Vietnam · 🇮🇩 Indonesia · 🇹🇭 Thailand · 🇲🇾 Malaysia

ASEAN neighbors invested in fiber infrastructure YEARS ago

Globe/Smart catching up with fiber rollout but still far behind

Part 3

CAUSES OF THE
DIGITAL DIVIDE

It's Not One Factor — It's a Web

05Cause 1

Economic Factors 💰

Internet is a luxury expense for many Filipinos:

• Fiber connection: ₱1,500-2,500/month

• Decent smartphone: ₱8,000-15,000

• Laptop: ₱20,000-40,000

• Minimum wage (NCR): ₱610/day = ₱15,860/month

05Cause 2

Geographic & Infrastructure Factors 🏝️

🏝️ 7,641 islands — laying fiber optic cables across water is expensive

⛰️ Mountainous terrain makes cell towers difficult to build

💼 Telcos prioritize profitable urban areas first

🌀 Typhoons regularly destroy infrastructure in provinces

05Cause 3

Social & Demographic Factors

Who's most excluded:

• Elderly (less tech-savvy)

• Women in rural areas

• Persons with disabilities

• Indigenous peoples

Why:

• Content mostly in English

• Platforms not accessible (PWD)

• Cultural barriers to adoption

05Cause 4

Education & Literacy Factors 📚

Digital literacy ≠ knowing how to use Facebook

Real digital literacy means:

Evaluating sourcesUsing productivity toolsProtecting privacyCreating contentBasic codingOnline collaboration
Part 4

CONSEQUENCES

The Digital Divide Determines Life Outcomes

06Education

Impact on Education

📚 Can't access online learning platforms, resources, libraries

📝 Can't submit digital requirements on time

📊 3.2 million PH students dropped out during pandemic

💰 Education gap → lifetime earnings gap (cycle of poverty)

06Employment

Impact on Employment

💼 Most job applications now online (no access = can't apply)

🏠 Remote work requires fast internet (WFH boom left many behind)

🖥️ Digital skills required even for basic jobs now

⚠️ No access/skills = limited to low-wage, in-person work only

06Health & Gov

Impact on Healthcare & Gov Services

🏥 Healthcare

• Telemedicine needs internet

• Health apps need smartphones

• Health info primarily online now

🏛️ Government

• PhilSys, SSS, Pag-IBIG → online

• E-governance excludes offline citizens

• Digital IDs require connectivity

06Democracy

Impact on Democracy & Information

📰 News is primarily online now — less access = less informed

🤔 Can't fact-check → more vulnerable to misinformation/fake news

🗳️ Digital civic participation limited (online petitions, consultations)

⚠️ Information asymmetry = power asymmetry

Part 5

BRIDGING
THE DIVIDE

Solutions From All Sectors

07Government

Government Initiatives 🇵🇭

📡 Free WiFi in public spaces (RA 10929)

📱 DITO Telecommunity — 3rd major telco (more competition)

🌐 National Broadband Program — fiber backbone to provinces

💻 DepEd laptop distribution for teachers and students

🏝️ DICT connectivity projects in GIDAs

07Key Law

RA 10929: Free Internet in Public Places

Enacted: 2017 — Free Internet Access in Public Places Act

Mandates free WiFi in:

Gov't OfficesPublic HospitalsHealth CentersTransport TerminalsParks & PlazasPublic Libraries

Implementation: Ongoing but slow. Many areas still not covered.

07Private & NGO

Private Sector & NGO Efforts

📡 Telcos

• Globe/Smart expanding 5G & fiber

• Starlink entering PH (satellite)

• Community WiFi via barangays

🤝 NGOs

• World Vision: school connectivity

• Tech companies donating devices

• Digital literacy training programs

07Solutions

What Can Be Done: Solutions

  • Invest in fiber infrastructure to provinces (not just NCR)
  • Subsidize internet/devices for low-income families
  • Integrate digital literacy into K-12 curriculum
  • Develop Filipino-language digital content
  • Create community tech hubs (barangay digital centers)
  • Promote telco competition to drive prices down
08Summary

Key Takeaways 🔑

  • Digital divide = access + skills + outcomes gap
  • PH: 73% online but massive urban-rural inequality
  • Causes: economic, geographic, social, educational
  • COVID exposed the crisis (3.2M students dropped out)
  • RA 10929 mandates free public WiFi (slow implementation)
  • Solutions need government + private + community action
