What is Green Technology?

The use of science to reduce environmental impact and promote sustainability through eco-friendly energy, materials, processes, and products.

Also called: clean technology, environmental technology, sustainable technology

04Solar

Solar Energy ☀️

How it works:

• Photovoltaic cells convert sunlight to electricity

• Cost dropped 89% since 2010

• Modular: rooftop to utility-scale

Philippines:

• High solar irradiance (tropical!)

• 1,500+ MW installed capacity

• Net metering available (sell excess)

04Wind

Wind Energy 💨

How it works:

• Wind spins turbine blades

• Generator converts rotation to electricity

• Onshore and offshore variants

Philippines:

• Bangui Wind Farm, Ilocos Norte (2005)

• 443 MW installed capacity

• Best areas: Ilocos, Eastern Visayas

04Hydro & Geo

Hydropower & Geothermal

💧 Hydropower

• Flowing water spins turbines

• PH: Agus-Pulangi complex (Mindanao)

• Reliable but ecosystem concerns (dams)

🌋 Geothermal

• Earth's internal heat → steam → electricity

• PH is 3rd largest producer globally!

• Tiwi, Makiling-Banahaw, Leyte plants

Rethinking How We Make & Use Things ♻️

05Comparison

Linear vs Circular Economy

❌ Linear (Old Way)

Extract → Make → Use → THROW AWAY

One-way trip to landfill. Wasteful.

✅ Circular (New Way)

Design → Use → Repair → Reuse → Recycle → Remake

Closed loop. Zero waste goal.

05Principles

Circular Economy Principles

🚫

Design Out Waste

Eliminate waste & pollution from the design stage

🔄

Keep Materials in Use

Repair, reuse, remanufacture, recycle

🌱

Regenerate Nature

Return nutrients to the soil, restore ecosystems

05Real World

Circular Economy Examples

🧥 Patagonia

Repairs old jackets, resells used gear, recycles materials

🍎 Apple (Daisy Robot)

Disassembles 200 iPhones/hour to recover materials

📦 Loop

Delivers products in reusable containers, collects empties

♻️ TerraCycle

Turns "unrecyclable" waste into new products

05Connection

E-Waste & Circular Economy

Circular economy is the answer to the e-waste crisis:

✅ Design phones for easy disassembly

✅ Recover precious metals (gold, silver, rare earth)

✅ Refurbish and resell (certified pre-owned)

✅ EU already mandates repairable, replaceable batteries

Part 4

PH ENVIRONMENTAL
POLICIES

The Laws We Have (and Struggle to Implement)

07Law 1

RA 9729: Climate Change Act (2009)

• Created the Climate Change Commission (CCC)

• Mandates mainstreaming climate change into government planning

• Framework for National Climate Change Action Plan (NCCAP)

• Focuses on adaptation & mitigation strategies

07Law 2

RA 9003: Ecological Solid Waste (2000)

• Mandates waste segregation at source

• Requires recycling & composting programs

• Bans open dumpsites (still not fully enforced)

• Establishes Materials Recovery Facilities (MRFs) per barangay

07Law 3

RA 8749: Clean Air Act (1999)

• Sets national air quality standards

• Regulates vehicle emissions (emission testing)

• Bans incineration (except for specific cases)

• Created EMB air quality monitoring network

07More Laws

Other Key PH Environmental Laws

RA 9275 — Clean Water Act (2004)

RA 9367 — Biofuels Act (2006)

RA 9513 — Renewable Energy Act (2008)

EO 174 — PH Greenhouse Gas Inventory

We have the laws. Implementation is the real challenge. 🇵🇭

08Reality Check

Challenges to Green Tech in PH

  • High upfront costs (solar panels, EVs still expensive)
  • Lack of infrastructure (few EV charging stations)
  • Weak policy implementation and enforcement
  • Corruption in environmental agencies
  • Low public awareness and education
  • Power of fossil fuel industry lobbies
