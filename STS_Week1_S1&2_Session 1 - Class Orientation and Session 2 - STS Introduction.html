01 Defining the Terms

What is Science?

A systematic enterprise that builds and organizes knowledge in the form of testable explanations and predictions about the universe.

From Latin scientia = "knowledge"

12 / 65
01 Branches

Science has many faces

🔬 Natural Sciences

Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Earth Science

🧮 Formal Sciences

Mathematics, Logic, Statistics, Computer Science

👥 Social Sciences

Psychology, Sociology, Economics, Political Science

All driven by the scientific method: observe → hypothesize → test → conclude

13 / 65
01 Defining the Terms

What is Technology?

The application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes. It includes tools, machines, systems, and methods used to solve real-world problems.

From Greek techne = "art, skill, craft" + logos = "word, study"

02 Mesopotamia 🏺

The Cradle of Civilization

☸️ The Wheel (3500 BCE)

Originally for pottery, later revolutionized transportation and trade.

✍️ Cuneiform Writing (3400 BCE)

First writing system. Enabled record-keeping, laws, and literature.

🌾 Irrigation Systems

Controlled flooding for agriculture. Fed growing cities.

📐 Base-60 Math

Why we have 60 seconds/minute and 360° in a circle!

38 / 65
02 Ancient Egypt 🔺

Masters of Engineering

🔺 Pyramids of Giza

Built ~2560 BCE with precision that still puzzles engineers today. 2.3 million stone blocks.

📜 Papyrus

First portable writing material. The ancestor of paper and books.

💀 Mummification

Advanced knowledge of chemistry and human anatomy. Early medical science.

📅 365-Day Calendar

Based on Nile flooding and star observations. Still the basis of ours.

02 Ancient China 🐉

The Four Great Inventions

📄 Paper (105 CE)

Cai Lun. Made knowledge portable and shareable.

🖨️ Printing (868 CE)

Woodblock printing → movable type. Mass communication begins.

💥 Gunpowder (9th century)

Originally for fireworks. Later changed warfare forever.

🧭 Compass (11th century)

Enabled global navigation and the Age of Exploration.

44 / 65
02 Ancient India 🇮🇳

Mathematics & Medicine

0️⃣ Zero & Decimal System

Aryabhata & Brahmagupta. Without zero, no computers, no programming, no modern math.

🏥 Ayurveda & Surgery

Sushruta (600 BCE): "Father of Surgery." Performed rhinoplasty, cataract surgery.

🔢 Algebra

Indian mathematicians solved quadratic equations centuries before Europe.

🏗️ Urban Planning

Indus Valley: grid streets, sewage systems, public baths (2600 BCE).

45 / 65
02 Islamic Golden Age 🕌

750-1258 CE: Knowledge Explosion

🔬 Optics

Ibn al-Haytham: "Father of Optics." Scientific method pioneer. Wrote Book of Optics.

🧪 Chemistry

Jabir ibn Hayyan: distillation, crystallization, acids. Basis of modern chemistry.

📚 Preservation

Translated Greek, Indian, Persian texts. Without them, ancient knowledge would be lost.

🏥 Hospitals

First public hospitals, pharmacies, and medical schools. Standardized treatments.

The "Dark Ages" Weren't That Dark

02 Game Changer 🔬

The Scientific Revolution (1543-1687)

A period when the scientific method replaced tradition and dogma. Observation and experimentation became the gold standard for knowledge.

The shift:

From "The Church says so" → to "Let me test it myself"

02 Spotlight

Galileo Galilei 🔭

CONTRIBUTIONS

  • Improved the telescope (30x magnification)
  • Discovered Jupiter's moons
  • Observed sunspots and lunar craters
  • Proved heliocentric model through observation

THE CONTROVERSY

  • Catholic Church: "Earth is the center!"
  • Galileo: "But I can SEE it's not..."
  • Placed under house arrest (1633)
  • Vindicated 350 years later by the Vatican

53 / 65
02 Tech That Changed Everything

Gutenberg's Printing Press 🖨️

Impact comparison: printing press vs. modern internet

BEFORE (hand-copying)

  • 1 book = months of work
  • Only monks had access
  • Information = power for elites

AFTER (printing press)

  • Mass production of books
  • Literacy rates skyrocketed
  • Reformation, science boom

Sound familiar? That's what the internet did to us. 🌐

02 Societal Impact

The Industrial Revolution changed EVERYTHING

👍 Positives

• Mass production lowered costs
• Transportation revolution (trains, ships)
• Rise of the middle class
• Medical advances

👎 Negatives

• Child labor exploitation
• Pollution and environmental damage
• Terrible working conditions
• Wealth inequality

Notice the pattern? Progress always has a cost. This is a core STS theme.

02 Key Insight 💡

What's the pattern?

  • Every major invention built on previous ones (standing on shoulders)
  • Technology accelerates over time (exponential growth)
  • Every breakthrough had BOTH positive and negative effects
  • Science and society always shape each other
  • The people who control technology control power
