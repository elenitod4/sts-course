01 Course Overview

What is this course about?

STS examines how science and technology shape society, and how society shapes science and technology in return. It's a two-way street.

This course is for EVERYONE:

BS Information Systems • Social Work • Education • Criminology • Business

  • Evaluate current ICT trends and emerging technologies

01 Defining the Terms

What is Science?

A systematic enterprise that builds and organizes knowledge in the form of testable explanations and predictions about the universe.

From Latin scientia = "knowledge"

01 Branches

Science has many faces

🔬 Natural Sciences

Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Earth Science

🧮 Formal Sciences

Mathematics, Logic, Statistics, Computer Science

👥 Social Sciences

Psychology, Sociology, Economics, Political Science

All driven by the scientific method: observe → hypothesize → test → conclude

01 Defining the Terms

What is Technology?

The application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes. Includes tools, machines, systems, and methods used to solve real-world problems.

From Greek techne = "art, skill, craft" + logos = "study"

01 Defining the Terms

What is Society?

A group of individuals involved in persistent social interaction, sharing the same territory, subject to the same political authority and dominant cultural expectations.

Society = people + culture + institutions + shared norms

02 Mesopotamia 🏺

The Cradle of Civilization

☸️ The Wheel (3500 BCE)

Originally for pottery, then revolutionized transport and trade.

✍️ Cuneiform (3400 BCE)

First writing system. Enabled record-keeping and laws.

🌾 Irrigation

Controlled flooding for agriculture. Fed growing cities.

📐 Base-60 Math

Why we have 60 seconds/min and 360° in a circle!

02 Ancient Egypt 🔺

Masters of Engineering

🔺 Pyramids of Giza

~2560 BCE. 2.3 million stone blocks with amazing precision.

📜 Papyrus

First portable writing material. Ancestor of paper and books.

💀 Mummification

Chemistry and anatomy. Early medical science.

📅 365-Day Calendar

Based on Nile flooding and star observations.

🤯

Fun Fact

Cleopatra lived closer in time to the Moon landing (1969) than to the building of the Great Pyramid (2560 BCE).

02 Ancient Greece 🏛️

The Birth of Western Science

🧠 Philosophy & Logic

Socrates, Plato, Aristotle: systematic questioning.

📐 Mathematics

Pythagoras, Euclid, Archimedes.

⚕️ Medicine

Hippocrates: evidence over superstition.

🌍 Astronomy

Eratosthenes calculated Earth's size.

02 Ancient China 🐉

The Four Great Inventions

📄 Paper (105 CE)

Made knowledge portable and shareable.

🖨️ Printing (868 CE)

Mass communication begins.

💥 Gunpowder (9th c.)

Changed warfare forever.

🧭 Compass (11th c.)

Enabled global navigation.

02 Ancient India 🇮🇳

Mathematics & Medicine

0️⃣ Zero & Decimal System

Without zero, no computers, no modern math.

🏥 Surgery

Sushruta (600 BCE) performed rhinoplasty.

🔢 Algebra

Quadratic equations centuries before Europe.

🏗️ Urban Planning

Indus Valley: grid streets, sewage (2600 BCE).

02 Islamic Golden Age 🕌

750-1258 CE: Knowledge Explosion

🔬 Optics

Ibn al-Haytham: scientific method pioneer.

🧪 Chemistry

Jabir ibn Hayyan: distillation, acids.

📚 Preservation

Translated Greek, Indian, Persian texts.

🏥 Hospitals

First public hospitals and pharmacies.

02 Game Changer 🔬

The Scientific Revolution (1543-1687)

When the scientific method replaced tradition and dogma. Observation and experimentation became the gold standard for knowledge.

From "The Church says so" → to "Let me test it myself"

02 Societal Impact

Both good and bad

👍 Positives

Mass production, transport revolution, middle class, medical advances.

👎 Negatives

Child labor, pollution, terrible conditions, wealth inequality.

Progress always has a cost. This is a core STS theme.

02 Key Insight 💡

What's the pattern?

  • Every major invention built on previous ones
  • Technology accelerates over time
  • Every breakthrough had BOTH positive and negative effects
  • Science and society always shape each other
  • Those who control technology control power
