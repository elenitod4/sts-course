17 definition

What is Biotechnology?

The use of biological systems and organisms to develop products, processes, or systems. From ancient fermentation to modern gene editing.

Not new (bread, beer, cheese = biotech). What's new is the PRECISION.

17 types

Colors of Biotechnology

🔴 Red (Medical)

Gene therapy, vaccines, diagnostics, personalized medicine.

🟢 Green (Agriculture)

GMOs, pest resistance, drought tolerance, yield improvement.

⚪ White (Industrial)

Biofuels, enzymes, biodegradable plastics.

🔵 Blue (Marine)

Aquaculture, ocean bioprospecting.

17 game changer

CRISPR-Cas9: Gene Editing

CRISPR = molecular scissors for DNA. Cut, delete, or insert gene sequences with precision. Cheap, fast, accessible. Can cure diseases or create designer organisms.

Exciting AND terrifying. The ethical stakes are enormous.

17 agricultural biotech

Biotech in Agriculture (GMOs)

✅ Benefits

Pest resistance, higher yields, drought tolerance, nutritional enhancement (Golden Rice).

⚠️ Concerns

Safety debates, corporate seed control, biodiversity loss, labeling issues, public distrust.

Philippines approved Bt corn and Golden Rice. Debate continues.

17 ethics bomb

Should We Edit Human Genes?

The case FOR

Cure genetic diseases (sickle cell, cystic fibrosis, Huntington's). End inherited suffering.

The case AGAINST

Designer babies, genetic inequality, unknown long-term effects, consent (embryos can't consent).

He Jiankui (2018): edited human embryos. Condemned globally. Imprisoned.

17 PH context

Biotech in the Philippines

🇵🇭 What's happening

DOST-BIOTECH program, Golden Rice approved, coconut genomics, marine biotech.

⚠️ Challenges

Public GMO skepticism, limited funding, slow regulation, poor science communication.

🧬😰🤔

Scientists: "We can cure genetic diseases!"

Public: "Are you playing God?"

Fear outpaces science. STS bridges the gap.

18 case study

COVID Vaccines: Biotech in Action

🔬 The Science

mRNA vaccines instruct cells to produce spike protein → immune response. No live virus needed.

🌍 The Impact

Developed in months (usually takes years). Saved millions. But: IP battles, access inequity, misinformation.

18 cloning & stem cells

Cloning & Stem Cell Research

🐑 Cloning

Dolly (1996): first mammal cloned. Raised questions about reproductive cloning in humans.

🧫 Stem Cells

Can become any cell type. Applications: organ growth, spinal injury repair, cancer treatment.

Ethics: embryonic stem cells vs. iPSCs (adult-derived, less controversial).

18 regulation

Biosafety & Regulation

🌍 International

Cartagena Protocol (2000): safe handling of GMOs across borders.

🇵🇭 Philippine

Joint Department Circular (DOST-DA-DENR-DOH). National Biosafety Framework.

Key principle: precautionary principle. When uncertain, be cautious.

18 bioethics

Four Principles of Bioethics

🙋 Autonomy

Respect individual choice and informed consent.

✅ Beneficence

Do good. Maximize benefits.

🚫 Non-maleficence

Do no harm. Minimize risks.

⚖️ Justice

Fair distribution of benefits and burdens.
