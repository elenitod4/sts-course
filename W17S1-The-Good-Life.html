Part 1

PHILOSOPHICAL
PERSPECTIVES

2,500 Years of Answers

03Hedonism

Hedonism: Pleasure as the Good

The good life = maximize pleasure, minimize pain.

• Simple and honest about human nature

• Problem: some pleasures DESTROY you (addiction, excess)

• Not ALL pleasure leads to a good life

03Epicurus

Epicurus: Simple Pleasures

NOT about excess! The HIGHEST pleasures are SIMPLE:

FriendshipGood FoodConversationFreedom from AnxietySimple Garden Life

Epicureanism ≠ excess. It's about SAVORING simple joys.

03Stoicism

Stoicism: Virtue & Inner Peace

The good life = virtue + inner peace regardless of circumstances.

• Control what you CAN (thoughts, reactions)

• Accept what you CAN'T (external events)

• A slave (Epictetus) AND emperor (Marcus Aurelius) both found peace here

03Existentialism

Existentialism: Creating Your Own Meaning

No inherent meaning — you must CREATE your own.

• "Existence precedes essence" — Sartre

• Radical freedom = radical responsibility

• YOU choose your values, purpose, identity. Scary but liberating.

03Utilitarianism

Utilitarianism: Greatest Good

The good life = producing the greatest good for the greatest number.

• Your life is good insofar as it CONTRIBUTES to others

• Service, impact, reducing suffering

• Very STS-relevant: technology SHOULD maximize collective well-being

Part 2

MATERIALISM
vs MEANING

Does Having More = Living Better?

04Psychology

The Hedonic Treadmill

Humans ADAPT to new levels of wealth & comfort:

📱 New phone → excited 2 weeks → normal

💰 Pay raise → happy 1 month → want more

🏃 Running on a treadmill: moving but never arriving

04Research

Does Money Buy Happiness?

Research says: YES, up to a point.

✅ Money solves real problems (food, shelter, health, education)

✅ Poverty DEFINITELY prevents well-being

⚠️ BUT after basic comfort is met, more money = diminishing returns

04PH Context

The ₱75,000/month Threshold

₱75K/month

Approximate threshold where additional income stops significantly increasing happiness in PH context.

₱22K/month

Median Filipino family income. For MOST, economic security IS directly linked to well-being. Poverty is real.

04Filipino Culture

Materialism & Filipino Culture

🚗 Pressure to SHOW success (new car, branded clothes, big house)

😰 "Nakakahiya" if you look poor — social pressure to spend

📱 Social media amplifies: showing off purchases for validation

💳 Utang culture: borrowing to look successful. Material success ≠ good life.

04Tech & Consumerism

Consumerism & Technology

🎯 Targeted ads know your desires better than YOU do

🛒 One-click purchases, buy-now-pay-later schemes

📸 Influencer culture: "Buy this to be like me"

Every app is designed to SELL you something. Is this YOUR good life or THEIR profit?

Part 3

THE
MEANINGFUL LIFE

Deeper Than Pleasure, Bigger Than Stuff

05Frankl

Viktor Frankl: Man's Search for Meaning

Survived Auschwitz. His insight:

"Those who have a WHY to live can bear almost any HOW."

Finding MEANING is the primary human motivation — not pleasure, not power.

05Sources

3 Sources of Meaning (Frankl)

🎨

Creative Values

What you GIVE to the world (work, art, service)

🌅

Experiential Values

What you RECEIVE (beauty, love, nature)

💪

Attitudinal Values

How you FACE suffering (courage, dignity)

05Japanese

Ikigai: Japanese Purpose 生き甲斐

Your "reason for being" — the intersection of:

❤️ What you LOVE

⭐ What you're GOOD AT

🌍 What the world NEEDS

💰 What you can be PAID FOR

05Purpose

The Purpose-Driven Life

Whether religious or secular, having PURPOSE correlates with:

📈 Greater longevity (+7 years on average)

💪 Better physical health & immune function

😊 Higher life satisfaction & resilience

🧠 Lower rates of depression & cognitive decline

05Filipino 🇵🇭

Filipino Sense of Purpose

Sources of Meaning for Filipinos:

👨‍👩‍👧 FAMILY — working hard FOR them

🙏 FAITH — purpose in God's plan

🤝 SERVICE — helping others (bayanihan)

💪 RESILIENCE — meaning even in hardship

Filipino culture naturally gravitates toward relational meaning — our sense of purpose is tied to the people we love and serve.

Part 4

GOOD LIFE IN
MODERN CONTEXT

Modern Movements Toward Living Well

06Balance

Work-Life Balance

🇯🇵 Japan's 'karoshi' (death from overwork) — the extreme warning

🧑‍💻 Gen Z pushes back: boundaries, mental health days, remote work

❓ Is "hustle culture" antithetical to the good life?

⚖️ The good life isn't ALL work. Rest is productive too.

06Less is More

Minimalism Movement

📦 Own LESS to live MORE — counter to consumerism

✨ Marie Kondo: "Does it spark joy?"

📱 Digital minimalism: intentional tech use (Cal Newport)

🎯 Less stuff = less stress = more time for what TRULY matters

06Intentional

Slow Living

🍳 Cook from scratch instead of fast food

🚶 Walk instead of rush

💬 Savor conversations, be present

🌟 Not lazy — INTENTIONAL. Your life ≠ your output.

Part 5

YOUR
GOOD LIFE

No Single Right Answer — But There Are Patterns

07Patterns

Different Paths, Same Goal

Common threads across ALL traditions:

1️⃣ Relationships matter MOST

2️⃣ Purpose/meaning beats pleasure

3️⃣ Excess destroys (moderation always)

4️⃣ Inner peace > external success

5️⃣ Contributing to others elevates YOUR life

07Design

Designing YOUR Good Life

Don't default to society's script. Ask yourself:

❓ What do I truly VALUE?

❓ What gives me ENERGY?

❓ Who do I want to BECOME?

❓ What will I regret NOT doing?

08Summary

Key Takeaways 🔑

  • Multiple valid philosophies: Hedonism, Stoicism, Existentialism, Utilitarianism
  • Hedonic treadmill: more stuff ≠ more happiness
  • Money helps up to ₱75K/month, then diminishing returns
  • Meaning (Frankl, Ikigai) provides deeper fulfillment
  • Filipino good life: relational, family-centered, faith-based
  • Design YOUR good life intentionally
