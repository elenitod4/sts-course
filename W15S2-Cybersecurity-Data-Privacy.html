02Distinction

Cybersecurity vs Information Security

🔒 Cybersecurity

Protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. Focus on TECHNOLOGY.

🛡️ Information Security

Protecting DATA integrity, confidentiality, and availability — digital or physical. Broader scope.

Part 1

TYPES OF
CYBER THREATS

Know Your Enemy 🎯

03Threat 1

Malware

Types:

🦠 Virus: Attaches to files, spreads

🐛 Worm: Self-replicates across networks

🐴 Trojan: Disguises as legit software

💰 Ransomware: Encrypts files, demands payment

Real Example:

WannaCry (2017)

• Hit 200,000+ computers

• 150 countries affected

• $4 billion in damages

03Threat 2

Phishing & Social Engineering

91% of cyberattacks start with phishing

📧 Fake emails: "Your GCash account has been locked, click here"

📱 Fake SMS: "You won ₱50,000! Claim now at bit.ly/scam"

📞 Vishing: Caller pretends to be bank employee asking for OTP

🎭 Social engineering: Manipulating PEOPLE, not systems

03Threat 3

Hacking & DDoS Attacks

💻 Hacking

• Unauthorized access to systems

• Data theft, vandalism, espionage

PH Case: 2016 COMELEC hack — 55M voter records exposed

🌊 DDoS

• Floods website with traffic

• Crashes the server/service

• PH gov't sites targeted multiple times

03Threat 4

Identity Theft & Online Fraud

🆔 Identity Theft: Using stolen personal info to commit fraud (open accounts, take loans)

🛒 Online Selling Fraud: Fake Shopee/Lazada sellers, never ship items

💸 Investment Scams: "Double your money!" Ponzi schemes (KAPA, etc.)

❤️ Romance Scams: Fake online relationships to extract money

03Threat 5

Cyberbullying & Online Harassment

😢 Cyberbullying: Using tech to harass, threaten, humiliate

📍 Doxxing: Publishing someone's private info publicly

📸 Revenge Porn: Sharing intimate images without consent

⚠️ Impact: Depression, anxiety, self-harm, suicide. Covered by RA 10175.

04Key Law

RA 10175: Cybercrime Prevention Act (2012)

The main Philippine law against cybercrimes.

• Signed into law: September 12, 2012

• Controversial: Online libel provision (free speech concerns)

• Enforced by: PNP Anti-Cybercrime Group (ACG) & NBI Cybercrime Division

04Provisions

RA 10175 Key Provisions

Offenses Against Confidentiality:

• Illegal access

• Data interference

• System interference

Computer-Related Offenses:

• Computer fraud

• Identity theft

• Cybersquatting

Content-Related Offenses:

• Cybersex

• Child pornography

• Online libel

Other Powers:

• Real-time data collection

• Preservation of data

• Search & seizure of digital evidence

04Penalties

Cybercrime Penalties

⚖️ Illegal Access: 6-12 years prison + fine

⚖️ Identity Theft: 6-12 years + ₱200K-500K fine

⚖️ Cybersex: 6-12 years + fine

⚖️ Online Libel: 6-12 years (one degree higher than RPC libel)

⚠️ These are REAL jail times. Cybercrimes are not jokes.

DATA
PRIVACY

Your Right to Control Your Information

05Definition

What is Data Privacy?

Your RIGHT to control how your personal information is collected, used, stored, and shared.

In the digital age, data is the new oil — companies WANT your data because it's worth billions. You deserve to protect it.

05Your Footprint

Your Digital Footprint

Everything you leave behind online:

Social Media PostsSearch HistoryOnline PurchasesLocation DataMessagesPhotosApp UsageBrowsing History

⚠️ Even "deleted" data may still exist on servers. You are the product.

05Key Law

RA 10173: Data Privacy Act of 2012

• Protects personal info in gov't AND private sector

• Applies to ANY processing of personal data

• Gives data subjects RIGHTS over their data

• Created the National Privacy Commission

• Penalties: Up to ₱5M fine + 3-6 years imprisonment

05NPC

National Privacy Commission (NPC)

🏛️ Independent body administering RA 10173

🔍 Investigates data breaches & privacy violations

📋 Handles complaints from data subjects

⚖️ Issues compliance orders & fines

📞 File complaints: privacy.gov.ph

05Your Rights

Rights of Data Subjects

📋 Right to be Informed

Know what data is collected & why

👁️ Right to Access

See & get copies of your data

🚫 Right to Object

Say NO to data processing

🗑️ Right to Erasure

Request deletion of your data

✏️ Right to Rectification

Correct errors in your data

📦 Right to Portability

Transfer your data elsewhere

05Principles

Data Privacy Principles

👁️

Transparency

Tell people WHAT you collect and WHY

🎯

Legitimate Purpose

Collect only for a valid, declared reason

⚖️

Proportionality

Don't collect more than you need

Part 4

PROTECTING
YOURSELF ONLINE

Practical Tips You Can Apply TODAY 🛡️

06Best Practices

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • 🔑 Use strong, unique passwords for each account
  • 📱 Enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) everywhere
  • 🔗 Don't click suspicious links or attachments
  • 🔄 Update software and apps regularly
  • 🌐 Use VPN on public WiFi
  • 💾 Back up important data regularly
06Passwords

Password Hygiene

✅ DO

• 12+ characters minimum

• Mix: upper, lower, numbers, symbols

• Use password manager (Bitwarden)

• Enable biometric login

❌ DON'T

• Reuse passwords across accounts

• Use personal info (birthday, name)

• Share passwords with anyone

• Save passwords in browser without master key

06Social Media

Social Media Safety

  • 🔒 Set profiles to PRIVATE
  • 📍 Don't share real-time location
  • 👔 Employers CHECK your social media
  • 🚫 Don't accept requests from strangers
  • 🆔 Never post: full birthday, address, school ID photo
  • ⚙️ Review app permissions regularly
06Spot Scams

Recognizing Scams & Phishing

🔍 Check sender email carefully (gogle.com vs google.com)

⏰ Watch for urgency: "ACT NOW or account deleted!"

🔗 Hover over links before clicking — check the REAL URL

🏦 Banks NEVER ask for passwords via email/text

✅ When in doubt → go directly to the official website/app

06If Hacked

What To Do If You're a Victim

1️⃣ Change ALL passwords immediately

2️⃣ Enable 2FA on all accounts

3️⃣ Report to PNP-ACG (Anti-Cybercrime Group)

4️⃣ File complaint with NPC for data privacy violations

5️⃣ Notify bank if financial data compromised

6️⃣ Document everything (screenshots as evidence)

07Summary

Key Takeaways 🔑

  • Cybercrimes rising fast: 60K+ cases/year in PH
  • RA 10175: Cybercrime Prevention Act (6-12 years prison)
  • RA 10173: Data Privacy Act (your rights over your data)
  • You have 6 rights as a data subject
  • Strong passwords + 2FA + awareness = best defense
  • Report to PNP-ACG and NPC if victimized
