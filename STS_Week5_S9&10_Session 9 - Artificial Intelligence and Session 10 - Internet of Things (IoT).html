09 definition

What is Artificial Intelligence?

Computer systems that perform tasks requiring human-like intelligence: learning, pattern recognition, language use, decision-making, and problem solving.

Key difference: traditional software follows rules. AI learns from data.

09 brief history

AI Timeline

1956 — Dartmouth Conference

AI as a field is born. Rule-based systems.

1970s-90s — AI Winters

Progress stalls. Hype exceeds reality.

2012 — Deep Learning breakthrough

ImageNet, neural networks beat humans at image tasks.

2017 — Transformer architecture

Foundation for GPT, BERT, modern language models.

2022+ — Generative AI era

ChatGPT, DALL-E, Midjourney go mainstream.

09 types

Types of AI

🎯 Narrow AI

Specific tasks only. All current AI. Spam filter, ChatGPT, face unlock.

🧠 General AI (AGI)

Human-level across all domains. Does NOT exist yet.

⚡ Superintelligence

Beyond human capability. Purely theoretical.

We have narrow AI. It's already transformative enough.

09 how it works

Machine Learning in 4 steps

1. Data

Feed the system large amounts of examples (text, images, numbers).

2. Training

System identifies patterns and relationships in the data.

3. Model

The learned patterns become a model that can make predictions.

4. Inference

Model processes new data and produces outputs (answers, classifications, content).

09 risks

AI Risks & Concerns

  • Bias: biased data → biased outputs (hiring, policing, lending)
  • Job displacement: routine cognitive work automatable
  • Deepfakes: synthetic media erodes trust
  • Privacy: surveillance at unprecedented scale
  • Power concentration: few companies control AI
  • Accountability: who's responsible when AI is wrong?
🤖📝🤔

Student: "Sir, is it okay to use AI for homework?"

09 jobs

AI and Employment

⚠️ Most exposed

Data entry, basic writing, customer support, some analysis, routine coding.

🛡️ Less exposed (for now)

Physical trades, emotional labor, creative judgment, complex negotiation.

AI replaces tasks, not entire people. But people who use AI will outpace those who don't.

Internet of
Things (IoT)

When everything connects, everything changes.

10 definition

What is the Internet of Things?

A network of physical objects embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity that collect and exchange data. Objects that were offline become data sources.

10 scale

How big is IoT?

75B+

$1T+

10 how it works

IoT Architecture: 3 Layers

1. 📡 Sensors

Collect data: temperature, motion, location, moisture, heart rate.

2. 🌐 Network

Transmit data: WiFi, Bluetooth, cellular, satellite.

3. ☁️ Platform

Process data: cloud analytics, triggers, automation.

10 examples

IoT in Real Life

🏠 Smart Home

Lights, locks, thermostat, cameras via phone.

⌚ Wearables

Fitness trackers, heart rate, sleep monitoring.

🏙️ Smart Cities

Traffic, waste, lighting management.

🏥 Healthcare

Remote monitoring, connected devices.

🌾 Agriculture

Soil sensors, auto-irrigation, drones.

🚚 Logistics

Package tracking, fleet management.

10 IoT + AI

When IoT meets AI = AIoT

IoT alone

Collects data from the physical world. Reports what's happening.

IoT + AI

Collects data AND makes intelligent decisions. Predicts, adapts, automates.

Example: soil sensor + AI = smart irrigation that waters exactly when and how much needed.

10 risks

IoT Risks & Concerns

  • Privacy: every device is a potential surveillance tool
  • Security: poorly secured devices = hacking targets
  • Data ownership: companies own your data, not you
  • E-waste: billions of devices = massive environmental cost
  • Dependence: system failures can cascade
🧊📡🕵️
