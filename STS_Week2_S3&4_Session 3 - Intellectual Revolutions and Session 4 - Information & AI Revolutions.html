03 warm up

What is an intellectual revolution?

An intellectual revolution happens when a new idea completely changes how people understand reality, knowledge, power, nature, or themselves.

Not just a new fact. A whole new way of thinking.

03 why this matters

Revolutions don't start with weapons. They start with ideas.

  • Ideas change beliefs
  • Beliefs change systems
  • Systems change societies
  • Societies change how technology is used

03 first revolution

The Copernican
Revolution

The moment humanity found out it wasn't the main character.

03 context

The old model: geocentrism

🌍 Earth-centered universe

Earth sat motionless at the center. Sun, moon, stars revolved around it.

⛪ Backed by authority

Supported by Aristotle, Ptolemy, and Church teaching. Challenging it was dangerous.

03 the man

Nicolaus Copernicus (1473-1543)

Polish astronomer who argued that the sun, not Earth, is at the center of the planetary system. Published De Revolutionibus in 1543.

03 the claim

Heliocentrism

The Earth moves. It rotates on its axis and revolves around the sun.

Sounds basic now. Back then, it was a mind grenade.

03 visualization

Geocentric vs Heliocentric

🌍

Geocentric

Earth at center. Knowledge from authority.

☀️

Heliocentric

Sun at center. Knowledge from observation.

03 impact

This revolution did 3 brutal things

  • Removed Earth from the center (we're not special)
  • Weakened blind trust in authority
  • Boosted observation, math, and evidence

03 second revolution

The Darwinian
Revolution

Humans stopped seeing themselves as separate from nature.

03 old belief

Before Darwin

Creationist fixity

Species individually created, fixed, unchanging.

Human exceptionalism

Humans wholly separate from animals in origin.

03 the idea

Evolution by natural selection

Species change over time. Traits that help organisms survive and reproduce become more common. No magic shortcut, just deep time plus selection.

03 4 steps

Natural selection, simplified

1. Variation

Individuals differ from one another.

2. Struggle

Resources are limited.

3. Selection

Useful traits increase survival.

4. Change

Populations evolve over generations.

03 the shock

Darwin's idea hit hard because...

  • It linked humans to other animals
  • It challenged literal religious interpretations
  • It reframed life as dynamic, not fixed
03 warning

Misuse: Social Darwinism

Some twisted Darwin's biology into bad politics, using "survival of the fittest" to justify racism and inequality. That's not science. That's ideology wearing a lab coat.

🧬🐒😑

Student: "So Darwin said I came from a monkey?"

Teacher: "No. Please respect both science and monkeys."

03 third revolution

The Freudian
Revolution

Humans realized their minds are not as transparent as they thought.

03 key idea

The unconscious mind

10%

Conscious

Thoughts you are aware of.

90%

Unconscious

Hidden drives, fears, memories, motives.

03 model

Id, Ego, Superego

🔥 Id

Primitive drives. Wants pleasure now.

🧠 Ego

Reality manager. Balances demands.

⚖️ Superego

Moral voice. Internalized rules.

03 nuance

Is Freud still accepted?

Still influential

Therapy, literature, film, pop psychology, behavior studies.

Also criticized

Hard to test, some ideas outdated, cultural bias.

Information & AI
Revolutions

From printed pages to machine-generated answers. Things escalated fast.

04 bridge

Why these count as intellectual revolutions

Because they changed how humans create, store, share, trust, and use knowledge. That's not a tech update. That's a civilization update.

04 definition

What is the Information Revolution?

A period where the production, processing, and distribution of information became radically faster, cheaper, and more powerful through computers, networks, and digital systems.

04 timeline

A fast history of information tech

1440: Printing press

Mass copying of ideas begins.

1830s: Telegraph

Messages travel faster than people.

1876: Telephone

Voice becomes instant over distance.

1940s: Computers

Machines process data at scale.

1990s: Internet

Global information network explodes.

2000s+: Smartphones

The network goes inside your pocket.

04 big shift

From scarcity to overload

then

Scarce, slow, expensive, gatekept.

now

Abundant, instant, cheap, often unreliable.

04 impact

What it changed

Education

Learning beyond classrooms.

Business

Data became the new fuel.

Politics

Opinion shaped at algorithm speed.

Crime

Cybercrime is a whole new field.

Social life

Identity moved online.

Work

Remote, hybrid, gig all normal.

04 dark side

Information also manipulates

  • Misinformation spreads faster than correction
  • Algorithms amplify outrage (it performs better)
  • Data collection turns users into products
  • Attention becomes a battleground
📚➡️📱😵
