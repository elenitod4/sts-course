07 so what?

What low R&D means for you

  • Fewer research jobs locally
  • More dependence on imported technology
  • More brain drain (talent leaves)
  • Innovations die from lack of support, not lack of talent
07 policy timeline

Key S&T policy milestones

1958 — National Science Development Board

First government body for science policy.

1986 — DOST established

Central S&T department created.

2007 — NICER Act

Research incentives for institutions.

2019 — Philippine Space Agency (PhilSA)

National space program formalized.

2022 — Innovative Startup Act

Support for tech startups and incubators.

⚠️ The barriers

07 sector: health

S&T in Philippine Healthcare

💪 Strengths

World-class health workers, DOST local test kits and ventilators, growing telemedicine (eConsulta, KonsultaMD).

⚠️ Gaps

Underfunded public health, rural access lacking, patchy digital infrastructure, equity issues.

07 sector: disaster

🌋 PHIVOLCS

07 sector: education

EdTech in the Philippines

📱 What's growing

LMS platforms, online classes, digital modules, AI tutors, DepEd Commons.

📵 The divide

07 critical issue

The Digital Divide 📵

  • Internet speed among lowest in ASEAN
  • Rural areas severely underserved
  • Mobile data expensive vs. income
  • Digital literacy varies widely
  • Millions excluded from tech-dependent services
📶🏔️😤

Student in Manila: *Zoom class, stable WiFi*

Student in province: *climbing a hill for 1 bar of signal*

Same country. Different realities.

07 program spotlight

Balik Scientist Program

DOST's initiative to bring Filipino scientists abroad back to PH for research, mentoring, and capacity building. Addresses brain drain by creating a bridge between diaspora talent and local institutions.

Not perfect, but a smart bridge between global talent and local need.

07 talent pipeline

Phil. Science High School System

What it is

DOST-run schools nurturing S&T talent. Free tuition, stipends, excellent facilities.

Impact

Graduates become scientists, engineers, researchers. Model works but needs more campuses.

🇵🇭🔬💸

Philippines: "We need more scientists!"

Also PH: *teacher salary barely covers rent*

The pipeline problem is real.

08 session 8

Filipino Innovations
Deep Dive 🇵🇭

Real innovators solving real Filipino problems.

08 framing

What makes innovation "Filipino"?

🎯 Local problems

Typhoons, off-grid communities, dengue, food security, island logistics.

🛠️ Appropriate tech

Uses local materials, designed for resource constraints, fits the context.

08 innovation 1

SALt Lamp — Aisa Mijeno 🔦

Problem: Coastal communities without electricity use dangerous kerosene.
Solution: Galvanic cell using saltwater to power LED + phone charging.
Impact: Safe, cheap lighting. International awards. Distributed to off-grid areas.

08 innovation 2

Diwata Satellites 🛰️

🛰️ What

Filipino-built microsatellites for remote sensing: agriculture, disaster, environment.

👩‍🔬 Who

UP & DOST engineers trained in Japan (Tohoku, Hokkaido).

📅 When

Diwata-1 (2016), Diwata-2 (2018). Deployed from ISS.

🏆 Result

Proved PH can do space science. Led to PhilSA creation.

08 innovation 3

Dengue OviTrap 🦟

Problem: Dengue kills hundreds yearly. Mosquito breeding is widespread.
Solution: Low-cost trap using dark container + water + sticky lining. Attracts and catches Aedes before breeding.
Impact: Community-deployable, cheap, requires no electricity or chemicals.

08 innovation 4

STARBOOKS — Digital Library 📚

📦 What

Standalone server preloaded with science books and journals. Works WITHOUT internet.

🏫 Where

Deployed to remote schools, libraries, barangay halls across the Philippines.

No internet? No problem. Knowledge should not be gated by connectivity.

08 innovation 5

Liter of Light 💡

What: Recycled plastic bottles + water + bleach refract sunlight into dark homes. Solar versions for nighttime.
Who: Illac Diaz, Filipino social entrepreneur.
Impact: Deployed in 15+ countries. Ultra low-cost, community-installable.

08 innovation 6

E-Jeepney & E-Trike 🚐⚡

✅ Benefits

Reduced emissions, lower fuel costs, modernized fleet, better passenger comfort.

⚠️ Challenges

Driver displacement fears, charging infrastructure gaps, high upfront cost for operators.

08 pattern

The pattern of Filipino innovation

  • Starts with a LOCAL problem
  • Uses locally available resources
  • Science is often simple but applied creatively
  • Context awareness > complexity
  • Impact measured by lives improved, not patents
🚀🔦🧂

Engineers abroad: *building rockets*

Filipino engineers: *lamp from salt and water because 12M have no electricity*

Both genius. Different contexts.

08 reality check

Why good innovations struggle to scale

  • Funding gaps after prototype stage
  • Bureaucracy and slow procurement
  • Lack of local manufacturing
  • Cheap imported alternatives undercut
  • Market education (people don't know it exists)
