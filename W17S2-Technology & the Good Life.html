10 Philosophy of Work

What Philosophers Say About Work

Marx: Alienation

When work is meaningless and exploitative, it alienates us from our human nature. Meaningful labor = flourishing.

Aristotle: Contemplation

The highest human activity is intellectual contemplation, not labor. Work is a means, not the end.

Stoics: Duty

Any work done with excellence and virtue contributes to the good life. The task doesn't matter, the attitude does.

Buddhist: Right Livelihood

Work that doesn't harm others and contributes to wellbeing. Part of the Eightfold Path.
