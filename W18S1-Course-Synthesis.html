02 Block 1: Foundations (Weeks 1-3)

STS & Intellectual Revolutions

Big Ideas:

  • 🔬 STS = Science ↔ Technology ↔ Society (they shape each other)
  • 🌍 Copernicus: Earth isn't the center
  • 🐒 Darwin: Humans aren't separate from nature
  • 🧠 Freud: We're not fully rational beings

Key Lesson:

Every intellectual revolution DISPLACED humans from the center. Each one was resisted by society but ultimately transformed it.

FINAL QUIZ 🧠

5 Questions · Comprehensive Review

Q1 Comprehensive Quiz

Name the 3 major intellectual revolutions and their impact:

A) Industrial, Digital, and Green revolutions
B) Copernican, Darwinian, and Freudian revolutions
C) French, American, and Russian revolutions
D) Agricultural, Scientific, and Information revolutions
✅ B) — Each displaced humans from centrality: Earth not center (Copernicus), humans not special (Darwin), we're not fully rational (Freud).
Q2 Comprehensive Quiz

What distinguishes AI from traditional software?

A) AI is faster than regular software
B) AI uses the internet while traditional software doesn't
C) AI learns from data and improves; traditional software follows fixed rules
D) AI only works on powerful computers
✅ C) — Machine learning allows AI to identify patterns and improve performance without being explicitly programmed for each scenario.
Q3 Comprehensive Quiz

The "digital divide" refers to:

A) The difference between digital and analog technology
B) The competition between tech companies
C) The divide between hardware and software
D) The gap in access to AND meaningful use of technology between groups
✅ D) — It includes access (devices, internet), skills (digital literacy), and meaningful use (productive vs passive consumption).
Q4 Comprehensive Quiz

The longest study on happiness (Harvard, 85 yrs) found:

A) Quality of close relationships is the #1 predictor of life satisfaction
B) Wealth is the strongest predictor of happiness
C) Career achievement matters most for wellbeing
D) Physical health is the only significant factor
✅ A) — 85+ years of data consistently show that warm, deep relationships predict health and happiness more than any other factor.
Q5 Comprehensive Quiz

The CORE purpose of STS as a discipline is:

A) To make students better at using technology
B) To ensure science & tech serve human flourishing and social good
C) To prepare students for tech careers only
D) To memorize the history of scientific discoveries
✅ B) — STS critically examines how science, technology, and society interact to ensure technology serves humanity, not the other way around.
