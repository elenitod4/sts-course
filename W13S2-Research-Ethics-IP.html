02 The Why

Why Research Ethics Matter

Research without ethics = exploitation. History showed us that unchecked science leads to suffering.

Ethics protects: human dignity, participant safety, scientific integrity, and public trust.

02 Case Study

Tuskegee Syphilis Study (1932-1972)

Duration: 40 years (1932-1972)

Subjects: 600 Black men in Alabama

Violation: No informed consent, withheld treatment

Deaths: 128 from syphilis/complications

Legacy: Belmont Report (1979) & modern IRBs

⚠️

NEVER AGAIN

02 Case Study

Milgram Obedience Experiment (1961)

The Setup

• "Teacher" told to shock "learner" (actor)

• Shocks increased to 450V (fake but they didn't know)

• Authority figure insisted "continue"

The Results

• 65% went to maximum 450V

• Extreme stress, trembling, crying

• Proved: people obey authority even when it's harmful

Ethical violations: deception, psychological harm, no real consent to the actual study

03 Requirements

What Makes Valid Consent?

🤝

VOLUNTARY

No coercion, threats, or undue influence. Free to say NO.

📋

Full disclosure: purpose, procedures, risks, benefits, alternatives.

COMPETENT

Capacity to understand & decide. Minors need parental consent + assent.

04 Definition

What is Intellectual Property?

Creations of the mind — inventions, literary works, artistic works, designs, symbols, names — given legal exclusive rights to the creator.

Your thesis, your code, your artwork = YOUR intellectual property 💡

04 Overview

4 Types of Intellectual Property

©️ Copyright

Creative works (books, music, code, art)

🔬 Patent

Inventions (products, processes)

™️ Trademark

Brand identity (logos, names, slogans)

🔒 Trade Secret

Confidential business info (formulas, methods)

05 Type 1

Copyright ©️

What it protects:

• Books, articles, thesis papers

• Music, films, photographs

• Software & source code

• Artwork & designs

Key facts:

• AUTOMATIC upon creation

• No registration required

• Duration: Life + 50 years (PH)

• Protects expression, not ideas

05 Type 2

Patent 🔬

Requirements:

• Novel (new, never before)

• Useful (has practical application)

• Non-obvious (inventive step)

Key facts:

• MUST REGISTER (unlike copyright)

• Duration: 20 years from filing

• Then becomes public domain

05 Type 3

Trademark ™️

What it protects:

• Brand names (Jollibee, Globe)

• Logos & symbols (Nike swoosh)

• Slogans ("I'm lovin' it")

Key facts:

• Must register with IPOPHL

• Renewable every 10 years

• Can last FOREVER if renewed

05 Type 4

Trade Secret 🔒

Famous examples:

• Coca-Cola formula (130+ years secret)

• KFC Original Recipe (11 herbs & spices)

• Google's search algorithm

Key facts:

• NO registration

• Protected as long as it stays secret

• Once leaked = protection GONE forever

06 Philippine Law 🇵🇭

RA 8293: IP Code of the Philippines

Enacted: January 1, 1998

• Established IPOPHL (Intellectual Property Office of the Philippines)

• Governs: Copyright, Patents, Trademarks, Industrial Designs, Utility Models

• Aligned with international treaties (TRIPS, Berne Convention, Paris Convention)

• Penalties: Fines ₱50K-₱1.5M + imprisonment 1-9 years for violations

07 Types

Types of Plagiarism

1.Direct Copying — word-for-word without quotes or citation
2.Paraphrasing — rewording without citing the source
3.Mosaic/Patchwork — mixing phrases from multiple sources
4.Self-plagiarism — resubmitting your own previous work
5.Ghost-writing — someone else writes it for you
07 Solutions

How to Avoid Plagiarism

✅ DO THIS

• Always cite sources (APA/MLA)

• Use quotation marks for direct quotes

• Paraphrase = change structure AND words

• Track references while researching

❌ DON'T DO THIS

• Copy-paste without citation

• Change a few words and call it yours

• Submit old papers to new classes

• Pay someone to write for you

09 Exception

Fair Use Doctrine

Limited use of copyrighted material WITHOUT permission for:

CriticismCommentNews ReportingTeachingScholarshipResearch

4 Factors: Purpose of use · Nature of work · Amount used · Effect on market value

⚠️ Teaching use ≠ unlimited use. Fair use has limits!

09 Alternative

Creative Commons Licenses

CC BY

Attribution only. Use freely, just credit the author.

CC BY-SA

Share Alike. Must license derivatives the same way.

CC BY-NC

Non-Commercial. Free for non-profit use only.

CC0

Public Domain. No rights reserved. Use for anything!

Pro tip: Use CC-licensed images in your presentations and papers! 📸

11 Before You Submit

Ethical Research Checklist ✅

  • ☐ Ethics clearance obtained (if human subjects)
  • ☐ Informed consent forms signed
  • ☐ All sources properly cited (APA/MLA format)
  • ☐ Plagiarism check below threshold (<20%)
  • ☐ Data is authentic (not fabricated/falsified)
  • ☐ All collaborators credited appropriately
  • ☐ Images/media are CC-licensed or self-made
